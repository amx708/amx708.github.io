莫尼什：今天能在此发言是我的荣幸，能以任何形式参与并和TiE合作始终是我的乐事。我对TiE怀有极大的敬意，深爱其使命。正如我的偶像查理·芒格所言，像好市多这样的企业对世界的贡献甚至远超洛克菲勒基金会。若深入剖析这句话，其核心在于创业者与创业精神才是推动世界运转的引擎。正是这种精神最终为社会创造了诸多福祉——从就业到经济增长等方方面面。一切始于创业者怀揣看似不可能实现的梦想。正因这与TiE的关切和关注点高度契合，让人自然而然地喜爱TiE，这实在令人欣喜。今天我想分享几点思考。希望有时间听取各位的见解，解答关于主题的疑问。我们将探讨我鲜少涉及的领域——销售与营销。和在座许多人一样，我出身工程师，接受过工程师训练。本科攻读工程专业时，我曾带着有色眼镜看待市场营销专业的同学，当时认为这门学科全是些浮夸的软知识，轻松得很，这些家伙根本没做过像我们工程师这样真正的硬核工作——诸如此类的刻板印象。二十出头时，我偶然读到一本书。今天我会提及几本著作。我准备了书单会分发给大家，不必记录我提及的书名。若各位想深入了解，我会将书单印在纸上分发。这大概是八十年代中期的事了。我读到威廉·戴维多夫所著的《高科技营销》，他如今是硅谷知名风投机构戴维多夫创投的合伙人。当时他原是英特尔研发主管，后被调任市场营销主管。这本书是他执掌英特尔营销数年后撰写的。

There are a couple of things he mentioned in the book that really stood out for me. One of the first things he said was that ‘marketing is an exact science, and it is almost as an exact science as physics. Obviously, that destroyed a lot of preconceived notions I had about the way I thought about marketing. Then he went about talking about what he called the laws of marketing, which in many ways, he thinks of as the laws of physics. Just to take a step back, this is 101, but marketing and sales are two completely different things. I will talk about sales a little later, but first I want to talk about marketing, and then we will dovetail into sale. Of course, there is some overlap, but there, I think of them as very distinctly different endeavors and I was intrigued by the way Davidow was talking about these laws of marketing. For example, one of his laws was that if you look at any market, any product, there will typically be three or four players at the most that control 80% of that market. It could be making airplanes, or it could be making hair clips. You can pretty much go across the board. It could be making software, anything you want. I found that stunning, and if you invert that, one of my heroes, Charlie Munger, says, every time you encounter something, always invert the logic to try to get some other place. If you invert that principle, first of all, if you agree with the principle, then the inversion would be that if you are starting a business and you say that this is a $10 billion market, and I just need 10% of it, and I will be a billion dollar company, Well, right there, you are off, because the inversion would be that if you are going after a market, your approach has to be, how do I get 60% of that market? If your starting point is that I am going to take 2% of this market, or 5% of this market, what you have not done is you have not segmented that market properly, and that is a fatal flaw. The first thing you ought to do is define the market that you are going after, and after you have defined it if you don't get to a very clear cut game plan to get 50, 60% market share, then don't even try because either you have not done your job correctly or you don't have anything to offer that is going to be that compelling to people. This is kind of one example of what Davidow was talking about and then I read a set of three books written by the same authors. The funny thing about these books is, all my marketing lessons which I still use today, were all from these books in the seventies and eighties. I am sure there is a bunch of newer one now, but I don't have those names to share with you because I am kind of locked into these core principles. But another set of three books was written by these two guys, Miller, and Heiman. The names of those books were Strategic Selling, Conceptual Selling, and Successful Large Account Management. Of course, even though the term “selling” is in those books, a large part of it has to do with marketing. Not so much about selling, I will get to selling later. Between Davidow and these Miller-Heiman books, I got a very solid education that marketing is not fluffy, it is really a set of very core rules. If you violate those rules, that would be like a physicist who claims that there is no gravity. The physicist can claim there is no gravity, but gravity is going to affect him, okay? So, you can think that there is no gravity, or it works a certain way or it works in reverse, but all of those things are going to come bite you. You really cannot ignore these core rules of marketing. If you don't want to ignore them, you at least must know them, right? It is hard to ignore something if you don't even know what it is. You must know these rules. I would say probably 90, 95% of entrepreneurs who get going do not understand these rules, and therefore the result is predictable. They are going to hit a brick wall soon and such.

他在书中提到的几件事让我印象深刻。他开篇就指出"营销是一门精确的科学，其严谨程度几乎可与物理学比肩"。这番话彻底颠覆了我对营销的既有认知。随后他阐述了所谓"营销法则"，在许多方面，他将这些法则视为物理定律的延伸。退一步说，这是最基础的认知：营销与销售是截然不同的领域。稍后我会谈及销售，但首先要阐述营销，然后再与销售衔接。当然两者存在交集，但我认为它们本质上是截然不同的领域。戴维奥阐述营销法则的方式令我着迷——例如他提出：任何市场或产品领域，通常仅有三到四家企业掌控着80%的市场份额。无论是制造飞机还是发夹皆是如此。这个规律几乎适用于所有领域，无论是软件开发还是其他行业。我对此深感震撼。而我的偶像查理·芒格则提出相反的观点：面对任何事物时，都要反向思考逻辑以开拓新思路。若认同这个原则并进行反向思考：当创业者宣称"这个十亿美元市场里，我只需10%份额就能成为十亿级企业"时—— 这想法本身就是错误的。因为反向思维要求：若要进军某个市场，你的思路必须是"如何拿下60%份额"。若起点是"我要占2%或5%市场"，说明你根本没做好市场细分，这是致命缺陷。首要任务是明确目标市场，若界定后仍无法制定出占据50%-60%份额的清晰战略，那就别费力气了——要么是你准备工作不到位，要么是你的产品缺乏足够吸引力。这正是戴维多所说的典型案例。后来我读了同一作者撰写的三部著作。有趣的是，我至今仍在运用的所有营销理念，都源自七十至八十年代的这些书籍。如今想必已有许多新著问世，但我无法向各位推荐具体书名，因为我始终坚守着这些核心原则。另一套三部曲则出自米勒与海曼之手，书名分别是《战略销售》《概念销售》和《成功的大客户管理》。尽管书名带"销售"二字，但内容很大程度上涉及营销领域——与销售本身关联不大，销售部分我稍后再谈。通过戴维奥和米勒-海曼的著作，我深刻领悟到：营销绝非空谈，而是由核心法则构成的体系。违背这些法则，无异于物理学家宣称引力不存在——纵使他如此主张，引力依然会作用于他，明白吗？所以，你可以认为重力不存在，或它以某种特殊方式运作，甚至反向作用，但这些想法终将反噬自身。营销的核心法则绝不容忽视。若不想违背这些法则，至少必须了解它们，对吧？连法则本身都不了解，又如何能刻意违背？你必须掌握这些规则。我敢说90%甚至95%的创业者在起步时都不理解这些法则，结果自然可想而知——他们很快就会撞上南墙。

The rules are very important. The second is that the stronger the marketing, the weaker the sales engine can be. I do not consider myself a good salesman. I consider myself a poor salesman. I consider myself a strong marketing person. If you have done your marketing homework, you can be a leper and make sales, and a standing example of that is someone in front of you. Because basically my approach to sales would be, 180 degrees approach to your traditional used car salesman schmoozing model and golf courses and all that. None of that is of much interest to me. The stronger the marketing, the less important the sales engine becomes. It is very important, in my opinion, to spend incredible amounts of time on the marketing aspect of the business, because if you don't spend that time on that aspect of it, you will spend 50x that time on the sales end and hit your head against a brick wall, and that is no fun. It is not fun to hit your head against a brick wall. It is good not to go there. I just feel the stronger the marketing, the weaker the sales.

规则至关重要。其次，营销越强，销售引擎可以越弱。我不认为自己是个好销售员，反而觉得自己是个差劲的销售员。我自认是营销高手。只要做好营销功课，就算是个麻风病人也能成交——眼前这位就是活生生的例子。因为我的销售方式与传统二手车销售员的套近乎模式、高尔夫球场应酬等做法截然相反。这些对我毫无吸引力。营销越强大，销售引擎的重要性就越低。在我看来，投入大量时间打磨营销环节至关重要。若忽视这部分，你将在销售端耗费五十倍精力却撞得头破血流——这种体验毫无乐趣可言。撞墙绝非乐事，最好别走这条路。我始终坚信：营销越强大，销售压力越小。

Now, the other aspect is that most things that people come up with is that they want to pitch to the world some basic rules. Charlie Munger’s rule, do not try to sell something that you wouldn't buy yourself. Okay, if you don't truly think it is something that you would yourself, be a customer of, don't try to pitch it. Don't be a used-car salesman. Make sure that whatever you are trying to come up with is compelling enough that you would yourself be deeply interested in buying. About 16 or 17 years ago, I read a book called Power versus Force, written by David Hawkins. When I refer to this book to my friends, I get two kinds of reactions. Either they are ready to throw it back at me and hope it really hurts me, or they love the book. I don't have people who say, “Ah, it was okay”. I get extreme reactions. I am sure when you read the book, if you decide to read it, you will have either one reaction where you will embrace it, or another where you say that this is such a bunch of nonsense. But please bear with me and just dive in and accept it. David Hawkins is not a marketing guy. He is a new age guru who hangs out in one of these new age places in Arizona with a ponytail and all.

现在，另一个方面是，人们提出的多数想法都试图向世界推销某些基本规则。查理·芒格的法则就是：不要推销自己不会购买的东西。没错，如果你不认为这是自己会购买的商品，就别去推销它。别当二手车销售员。确保你所推销的产品足够有吸引力，让你自己都产生强烈的购买欲望。大约16或17年前，我读过大卫·霍金斯所著的《力量与强制》。每当向朋友提及此书，总会引发两种极端反应：要么他们恨不得把书扔回我脸上，要么他们对这本书爱不释手。从没人对我说"啊，还行吧"。我得到的都是极端反应。相信当你决定阅读此书时，也必将陷入两极：要么欣然接受，要么斥之为无稽之谈。但请耐心读下去，先接纳它。大卫·霍金斯并非营销专家，而是位新世纪灵性导师——他扎着马尾辫，常出没于亚利桑那州的新世纪聚居地。

It is somewhat of a spiritual book. His thesis on that book is that if I lie to you, and in your conscious state, you do not know that I am lying to you, in your subconscious, you do. For all humans, there is a pipe, a communications highway or at least a one lane highway that is going between your subconscious and your conscious. Because of all the junk the world throws at us as we are growing up, for most of us humans, that pipe is almost completely clogged. Whatever your subconscious is trying to send as a message to you, to your conscious brain is barely able to get through. Usually, it gets through in some mumbled, jumbled way. There are certain humans for whom that pipe is less clogged than others. The less clogging that you have, the better judge of human nature and a better judge of the world, you are going to be, I believe that people like Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger have significantly less clogged pipes than most of the rest of us. I believe someone like Gandhi had almost a completely unclogged pipe and such. What David Hawkins said is that most of us, if someone is lying to you at the subconscious level, this guy is just feeding me a lie, and when your subconscious is trying to transmit that message to the conscious part to tell you get away from this idiot, it can't get through. But what happens is, a little bit of the signal for most of us does get through, and the little bit of the signal, what is telling you is you somehow feel that you don't feel that good being around that person. You may not know why you don't feel good about being around that person. For example, the classic use-car salesman, you really don't crave to be around that person, and you can't pinpoint which part of his pitch is a lie, but you generally don't want to be around that, and if you invert it, which is what Hawkins does, which is if someone is absolutely truthful to you all the time, you want to be as close to that person as much of the time as you can. Hawkins came up with what he called these attractor fields, and he put a bunch of math in the book as well, putting like a log scale on different attractor fields, which is probably where I lose some of my rational friends when they read the book. What he said is that humans have different levels of attractor fields that they emit. The more truthful you are, the stronger those attractor fields, the slimier used-car salesman you are, then you are just, like repelling people. They don't want to be around you and you are trying to chase them down and get them around you.

这本书多少有些灵性色彩。作者在书中的论点是：如果我对你撒谎，而在你清醒的意识状态下，你并不知道我在撒谎，但在你的潜意识里，你其实心知肚明。所有人类都有一条管道，一条连接潜意识与意识的沟通高速公路——至少是一条单行道。由于成长过程中世界向我们倾泻的种种垃圾，这条管道对大多数人而言几乎完全堵塞。无论潜意识试图向意识传递何种讯息，都难以完整抵达。通常它只能以含糊不清、支离破碎的方式穿透屏障。某些人的管道比他人通畅得多。我认为，管道越通畅，你对人性与世界的洞察力就越敏锐——像沃伦·巴菲特和查理·芒格这样的人物，他们的管道阻塞程度远低于常人。我认为像甘地这样的人，其管道几乎完全畅通无阻。大卫·霍金斯指出：当有人在潜意识层面欺骗你时，你的潜意识试图向意识传递"远离这个蠢货"的警报，却往往无法穿透阻塞。但实际情况是，对多数人而言，信号仍会残留微弱痕迹——这种微弱信号会让你隐约感到与对方相处时不太舒服。你或许说不清具体原因。比如经典的二手车销售员，你根本不想靠近他，却说不清他推销话术的哪部分是谎言——你就是本能地排斥这种人。反过来看，正如霍金斯所言：若有人始终对你绝对诚实，你就会想尽可能多地靠近他。霍金斯提出了所谓的"吸引力场"理论，并在书中运用大量数学模型——比如用对数尺度衡量不同吸引力场——这大概就是我那些理性派朋友读完书后产生困惑的原因。他指出人类会释放不同层级的吸引力场。你越是真诚，吸引力场就越强；你越像油嘴滑舌的二手车销售员，就越像是在排斥他人。人们不愿靠近你，而你却拼命追逐他们，想把他们拉到身边。

For example, he put Jesus, right at the absolute top of the attractor fields, absolute truth. He put Gandhi, I think probably, one whole log level down below Jesus. Then he put all kinds of other great people, Martin Luther King, and others kind of up there. Warren Buffett probably because he is a capitalist, is not quite like Gandhi, but he would be somewhere up there, right? You must give up discounts to capitalists, but basically, he would be up there. That is why people want to and that is why there are 40,000 people who show up in Omaha every year because it is those attractor fields that work. These attractor fields work, but Hawkins went further, and what he said is that if I lie to you, and we put these kinesiology sensors on you, not on me, on you that sensor is going to pick up changes in your muscles, which your subconscious is feeding into which will basically say you are being lied to. I have never tested that. I don't know whether that part of his thesis works or not, probably another reason why I lose my rational friends. But let us assume whether it works or not, the bottom line is, if I lie to you, your subconscious knows, and that is the end of it.

例如，他将耶稣置于吸引子场最顶端，视为绝对真理。甘地大概排在耶稣下方一个完整日志层级。其他伟人如马丁·路德·金等人也位列其中。沃伦·巴菲特虽因资本主义身份与甘地不同，但理应位居高位，对吧？资本家虽需让步，但本质上他仍属高位。这正是人们向往的原因，也是每年四万人涌向奥马哈的缘由——正是这些吸引子场在发挥作用。这些吸引力场确实有效，但霍金斯更进一步指出：若我对你撒谎，当你（而非我）佩戴肌动学传感器时，传感器会捕捉你肌肉的细微变化——这些变化源于潜意识传递的信号，本质上表明你正遭受欺骗。我从未验证过这个理论。我无法验证他理论的这一部分是否成立——这大概也是我失去理性派朋友的另一原因。但无论真伪，关键在于：当我欺骗你时，你的潜意识会知晓，事情到此便告终结。

Now, when you take that in the context of marketing, which is where I overlaid this. I am pretty sure the people at Procter and Gamble have never read Hawkins because the company predates Hawkins. But P&G has a principle. They have a principle that they will never market anything unless whatever they are saying is true, and it is genuinely an improvement. Whatever they are pitching must be true. What P&G has found probably over a hundred years of history of marketing everything, and they are the Gods of marketing, is, you cannot, in effect, put lipstick on a pig. If you have a substandard product, you dress it up well, what happens is, the human attractor fields just see straight through, and those products don't do well. What P&G has found is that if you are pitching some kind of detergent that is better than other detergents, it better be better. Otherwise, it just doesn't work. Someone like P&G really understands that at the core, and I think they may not realize it, but they really are kind of playing off Hawkins.
